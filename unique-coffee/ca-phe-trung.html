Cà Phê Trứng

Vietnamese Egg Coffee is a rich, creamy drink made with egg yolk, sugar, condensed milk, and strong Vietnamese coffee. The egg yolks are whipped into a frothy mixture, creating a custard-like texture that balances sweetness with bold coffee flavor. This coffee has a unique sweet and creamy flavor, combining the bitterness of strong coffee with the richness of whipped egg. The egg yolk contributes a rich, velvety texture, while the robusta coffee offers a strong, nutty flavor. The condensed milk adds sweetness, making the drink reminiscent of a dessert—a delightful treat for coffee lovers.

Egg coffee represents Vietnamese ingenuity and resourcefulness, born from a time of scarcity. It's not just a beverage; it’s a cultural icon that reflects the hospitality and warmth of Vietnamese traditions.This drink emerged in Hanoi during the 1946 milk shortage. Nguyen Giang, a bartender at the Sofitel Hotel, innovatively used egg yolks to replace milk in his coffee. His creation quickly gained popularity, leading to the establishment of Café Giang, which still serves egg coffee today.

How it’s Made

To prepare egg coffee, strong Vietnamese Robusta coffee is brewed. The egg yolk mixture is prepared separately by whisking egg yolks with sugar and condensed milk until frothy. The mixture is then gently folded into the brewed coffee, resulting in a thick, creamy layer on top.
