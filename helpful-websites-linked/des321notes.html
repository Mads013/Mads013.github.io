    • Class Overview

    Focus of Class:

    The initial 6+ weeks concentrate on Project 1, collaborating with Anna Zusman from Willamette Week to create a brand identity for the 2024 Best of Portland Readers’ Poll.

    Project Steps:

    The process involves interviewing the client, research, creating logos and brand assets, pitching work, and compiling everything into a final brand book.

    Group Work:

    Working in groups of 2-3 is mandatory for Project 1. All group members receive the same grade for the final project.

    Skill Development:

    Besides designing a functional campaign, the goal is to practice working in creative groups, emphasizing the importance of collaboration.

    Oral Presentations:

    Giving oral presentations is a key requirement, and finalists will present to real clients. Substantial research and effort outside class are expected.

    Design & Deliverables:

    The project includes creating a brand identity system, expressing the brand through various mediums, and developing a final Brand Book.

    Presentation Rounds:

    • Round One (Week 6):

      Small group presentations to instructors and classmates.

    • Round Two (Week 7):

      Panel presentations; 3-4 groups chosen to present to the client, with one group selected for further refinement.

    Brand Books:

    Each group is required to create a brand book, acting as guidelines for using the brand and showcasing design approaches and examples.

    Selected Finalists:

    The chosen group, with guidance, works for the remaining 4 weeks to refine and execute the chosen direction. Final outcomes are presented at the end of the term.

    Option for Project 2:

    Members of the chosen group have the option to work on Project 2 in addition to any edits on Project 1.

    Best of Portland Overview

    Willamette Week's Best of Portland Readers’ Poll

    Willamette Week's Best of Portland Readers’ Poll is an annual celebration of all things local. Readers are invited to celebrate and recognize people, businesses, services, and experiences in Portland by nominating and voting for them in a 3-month campaign to identify “the best” Portland has to offer.

    The full list of categories and subcategories is available here (note, these subcategories may change slightly in 2024).

    Project Overview

    Create a dynamic and memorable brand identity for the campaign that captures the essence of Portland's unique culture and fosters a sense of community pride.

    Brand Personality: The brand should embody the following traits:

    • Vibrant
    • Inclusive
    • Community-driven
    • Celebratory

    What the brand must do:

    • Brief! Stretch for Nominations, Voting, and Winners.
    • Must work as a “badge” for businesses to use easily other collateral. Easily recognizable and scalable.
    • Includes WW Logo, “Best of Portland Readers’ Poll”, Year, and Sponsor Logo (New Seasons).
    • Versatility: Brand must be adaptable across various mediums, including print, digital, and event collateral, while maintaining consistency and recognizability.
    • Stretch to reflect our categories: Arts & Entertainment, Food, Drink, Wellness + Self Care, Cannabis, People, Goods + Services.

    Target Audience: The primary audience is 1) the small business community, and 2) residents of Portland who care about supporting local - all ages, who actively engage in the city's cultural, culinary, and business scenes.
